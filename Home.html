The Lamina Emergent Torsional (LET) joint is a sophisticated yet versatile technology developed by BYU researchers. It involves a flat sheet of material with specialized segments. These segments serve as hinges, enabling controlled twisting movements, and they are interconnected by a component that maintains their alignment. View Technology page

The Corn Stalk Integration Device or "CID" is a fully functional agricultural technology developed at BYU. USing state-of-the-art electrode scanning technology, this device provides non-destructive analysis of inteior material integrity of Corn stalks. A functional gripping mechanism provides close contact with the corn stalk while a touchscreen interface provides logging of scan and gps data.
