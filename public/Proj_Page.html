LET Joint

Summary: The Lamina Emergent Torsional (LET) joint is a sophisticated yet versatile technology developed by BYU researchers. It involves a flat sheet of material with specialized segments. These segments serve as hinges, enabling controlled twisting movements, and they are interconnected by a component that maintains their alignment. The LET joint's applications are diverse. It can be used to create robust, flexible structures that can withstand significant bending. Additionally, it plays a pivotal role in micro-scale engineering, facilitating the construction of precise and flexible components like tiny hinges or flexible parts, adding a new dimension of capability to various applications.
